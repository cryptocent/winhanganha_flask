Winhanganha (win-HANG-ahn-ha)

Winhanganha means "To Know, Think, Remember" in the Wiradjuri language.

Although Winhanganha is a Wiradjuri word, this Archive is for all First Nations peoples.

What is the First Nations Winhanganha Archive?

The Archive is a curated digital repository of First Nations knowledge, language, story and history. It is reviewed and approved by First Nations Elders to support the preservation of culture for future generations.

Cultural Protocols

This archive contains culturally significant material. Some records may require restricted access, Elder approval or community consultation before viewing or reuse.

Items are reviewed by authorised library staff and community representatives before publication. Assessment considers cultural sensitivity, community permissions, access restrictions and appropriate metadata.

Users are expected to respect cultural ownership, community knowledge and access conditions attached to each item.

Access Levels

Items in the archive may be Public, Restricted, Under Review, or Private depending on cultural protocols and community approval.

User Access

Public visitors can browse approved records. Registered researchers may request access to restricted items. Elders and authorised reviewers can assess items before public release.

Request Access

Some records require approval before viewing. Users may submit a request for library staff and community reviewers to consider.
